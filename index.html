About me

Richard Xu

I've seen a lot and done a lot, but I feel like there's so much more to do.
Here's just a little about me:

  • 1995

    Game Start

    I was born in the quiet town of Walnut Creek, California. My parents had moved to the states a couple years ago, and were eager to start a family in the the technological bubble called Silicon Valley. My dad was a material Scientist and my mom a registered nurse, and this was the start of my adventure!

  • Sometime in 1998

    Portlandia

    Three years after I was born, my dad got relocated (thank you Fujitsu) and we moved to Beverton, a small suburb of Portland, Orgeon. In the state that was 99% forest, I grew to love the outdoors. I started discovering my interests as wide as they were, from soccer and swimming to playing with robots and gamming.

  • August 23,2004

    The day I became a cowboy

    But eventually, we had to move again, this time to Austin, Texas. I moved in 5th grade and I was that new kid in class. Luckily, southern hospitality and tons of other asian kids in my class gave me the opportunity to make many friends that I still talk to today, untill...

  • August 23, 2011

    Sleepless in Seattle

    I moved again. And this time, it sucked. I moved away from all my friends into the rainy, gloomy Bellevue, WA, near Seattle. But in retrospect, it wasn't that bad, I made new friends, and grew in my interest in chess, knowledge bowl, model United Nations, philosophy and robotics. Yeah, I think I've come to adore Seattle weather.

  • August 23, 2013

    Building, Berkeley and the Bay Area

    But nothing beats California sunshine, and that's where I am now, in sunny, liberal, beautiful Berkeley, California. I love being close to the bay and being back in Silicon Valley! And I look forward to my next few years here.

University of California, Berkeley

I perform the data analysis in the Language and Cognition Laboratory at UC Berkeley. My current project is to take the current set of TRPS (Topological Relation Picture Series) and expand the space by breaking down each scene into a set of features and comparing features across all scenes.

Lif3

Space Hackathon

Won the biologics in Space category of the Space Hackathon with a hardware hack that used an Intel Edison to perform data collection in space (in theory) and pushed the data to the Intel IoT servers and included a web app that allowed the user to query the cloud and pull down specific informations.

Xendit

BitBay Hackathon

Created the winning App in the Bitbay Hackathon, that transferred money across international boarders through the use of bitcoins. Our app took in fiat currency through local vendors and transferred it within 10 second to the destined country for instant conversion to local currency.

VYou

Play Hackathon

We made an app that judged sentiment for small businesses by scraping the web for reviews, such as Facebook and Yelp, and run a bootstrapped senitment analyzer to display on an easy to use interface. We didn't win, but it was a fun experience.

LeapMe

Cal Hacks

Lif3

Space Hackathon

During the Space Hackathon, I worked with a team to protoype a bioreactor that would operate in space. I think this was one of the more interesting projects I had to do, since most of our time was spent thinking about and designing our cube (bioreactor) rather than hacking away. The actual hacking was a bit difficult, since it was the first time any of us used an Intel Edison; however I focused more on the web design and creating an interface for people to query the cloud and pull data that the Edison sent.

  • HTML, CSS, Javascript
  • November 14-16, 2014
  • Space Hackathon
  • Web Design

ReUSE Website

I worked on the ReUSE website; ReUSE is a campus sustainability organization at UC Berkeley dedicated to divert reusable items from landfills and back into the Cal community!

I've been with ReUSE for both years I've been at Cal and it's been an exciting adventure. Each year we hold events and have projects to divert waste in order to reach Chancellor Dirk's goal of zero waste by 2020. We hold clothing sales, tye-dye events, move-out collections and many other events and divert over 1000 pounds of reusables each event.

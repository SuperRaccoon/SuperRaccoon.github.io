Committees

External Events Committee

The External Events committee plans external events and works with other student organizations such as TASA and KASA in organizing major events. Some of our awesome events in the past include Indomie Eating Competition, Party at Pappys, Melange Cultural Variety Show, and World Food Fair! Really looking forward and I hope you enjoy ISAB as much as we do!

Marketing Committee

The Marketing Committee is a great one! If you are interested in how people think, love to design or draw, or just love chatting with people, this committee is the coolest committee you're ever going to meet! In the marketing committee, we focus on using new and innovative ways to spread the word about ISAB events, as well as promoting international culture. We will focus on what it means to market events and an organization, as a whole. So, if you like people and like being creative, then this is going to be epic so join the marketing committee!

Social Committee

We're a tight-knit family here at ISAB, and one of the things that keep us so close is our social events. Every semester we plan events for our members to get to know each other and have a good time. You can look forward to dinner trips, social mixers, the iBig/iLittle mentorship system, well as our member retreat near Santa Cruz! Here at ISAB, we're all about having fun, and we don't want you to miss out. Come help us form life-lasting bonds as a member of the social committee!

Collaborative Events Committee

The collaborative events committee will focus on the general membership events and create a tightly knit and organized family system for everyone! We want as many international and domestic students to feel welcomed at ISAB, and we will be planning events such as a bowling night, ISAB professional alumni panel, Berkeley Project, and many other monthly events to have fun with all our general members.

International Program Committee

International Program Committee's responsibilities will mainly focus on hosting the "Meet the Bears" Summer Program. The "Meet the Bears" Program consists of current students and alumni preparing an orientation in their respective countries to help new incoming fall freshmen adjust to Berkeley. As specific and important our task is, the committee chair and members will be working closely together, coordinating the event with ASUC, Berkeley International Office (BIO), and International Alumni Relations. Furthermore, International Programs will serve as a liason between ISAB and BIO for cooperating on events such as iMixes. Hope we have fun together this semester!
